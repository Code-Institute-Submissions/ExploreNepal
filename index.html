ABOUT NEPAL

Nepal is a sovereign country in South Asia. It is mainly in the Himalayas, but also includes parts of the Indo-Gangetic Plain. It is the 49th largest country by population and 93rd largest country by area. It is landlocked, and borders Tibet in the north and India in the south, east and west. Nepal has a diverse geography, including fertile plains, subalpine forested hills, and eight of the world's ten tallest mountains, including Mount Everest, the highest point on Earth. Kathmandu is the capital and the largest city. Nepal is a multiethnic country, with Nepali as the official language. The most interesting fact is Nepal was never colonized by any other countries before. Gurkha or Nepalese Army is arguably the toughest soldier in the world and has a long establishment of relationships with the United Kingdom.

Maya Devi Temple, Lumbini

Widely known to the world as the birthplace of Lord Buddha, the Light of Asia, Lumbini is one of the most visited cities in Nepal where Maya Devi Temple is located. Maya Devi Temple is believed to be the house of Siddartha Gautama’s mother and currently is a landmark of UNESCO World Heritage Site. Maya Devi Temple has become the holy place for every Buddists and attracts various attention from around the world where many other countries have donated to build Buddist temples in the same complex to support the tourism of the country.

Gurkha Army

Gurkha Army is one of the strongest armies in the world and has become the pride symbol of Nepal. Gurkhas are closely associated with khukuri, a forward-curving Nepali knife, and have a reputation for military prowess. Through the Gurkha Army, Nepal has successfully established a great friendship with the United Kingdom and support in various different ways of the bilateral relationship.
