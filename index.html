ABOUT NEPAL

Nepal is a sovereign country in South Asia. It is mainly in the Himalayas, but also includes parts of the Indo-Gangetic Plain. It is the 49th largest country by population and 93rd largest country by area. It is landlocked, and borders Tibet in the north and India in the south, east and west. Nepal is a multiethnic country, with Nepali as the official language. The country enjoys mainly a temperate climate. The landscape is dominated by the Himalayan mountains in the North, alpine pastures, fertile valleys and temperate forests in the middle hill region and the Tarai plains in the South.Nepal is the largest country in the Himalayas and about the size of the state of New York/USA, about half the size of Equador and quite a bit bigger than Austria, Liechtenstein and Switzerland combined. To reach Nepal it takes a 13-hours flight to Kathmandu from London/UK or a 2-hours flight from Delhi/India. The shortest flights from the USA go via Dubai, Doha or Abu Dhabi and still take at least 21 hours!

The Everest

Home to the highest mountain peak, Mt. Everest is located in Nepal, 8,849 metres above sea level and attracted many mountain climbers over the years. Nepal has been tagged as the ‘ceiling of the world’ for being home to eight of the ten world’s highest mountains including Mount Everest. A significant portion of the Himalayas is in Nepal. Mount Everest was named after Sir George Everest.

Gurkha Army

Indian Chief of Army 'Sam Manekshaw' once said, “If a man says he is not afraid of dying, he is either lying or is a Gurkha.” Yes, Gurkha Army is one of the strongest armies in the world. Gurkhas are closely associated with khukuri, a forward-curving Nepali knife, no surprise but Nepal does not celebrate an independence day because they had never been under any foreign occupation.

The Birthplace of Buddha

Well, Nepal is a peaceful country because it is the birthplace of Budhha, Siddhartha Guatama or Lord Budhha was born in, Lumbini in 623 B.C. Lumbini has been declared as a World Heritage Site by UNESCO in 1997. Intrestingly Nepal is a Hindusim country in the Land of buddha and never faced any riots on the lines of religion or ethnicity.

Amazon of Asia

Nepal is the natural habitat to 5,980 species of flowering plants, 2% of the world's orchids and 6% of rhododendron species. Nepal shelters over 250 species of flora and fauna, 870 species of birds which is roughly the world’s 8%, 650 species of butterflies and the world's most giant moth and honeybees. Chitwan National Park is a reserve of endangered and rare animals such as Asia’s one-horned rhinoceros.

World Heritage Sites

If you think Nepal is a tiny country famous for mountaineering, you couldn’t be more wrong. The capital city, Kathmandu itself, has seven UNESCO World Heritage Cultural Sites within a radius of 15 km. and nicknamed as the living cultural museum of the world. Moreover, Nepal has four recognised World Heritage Sites by UNESCO.

Deepest and Highest

Housing the world’s deepest lake, Shey Phoksundo; the tallest grassland, the Chitwan; the deepest gorge, Kali Gandaki Gorge and harbours, the highest altitude lake at Lake Tilicho, Nepal has broken records for its enchanting natural beauty. Arun Valley, the uppermost valley on earth, too is in Nepal. It is the only country with such drastic altitude variation from 59 meters to 8,848 meters.

Newari Cuisines

Newari is the indigenous people of Nepal and they are the original inhabitants of Kathmandu Valley as well as the creators of the historic heritage and civilization of this beautiful region. The Newari are a mixed ethnic minority of Tibetans and Indians which drive the origin of Newar cuisines which are beautiful and delicious which indulge the tongues of people who taste it.
